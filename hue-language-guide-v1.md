# Hue Language Guide v1
*Single vocabulary reference for all Hue copy — product, observations, emails, sales, documentation.*
*All decisions here are resolved. Do not reopen without explicit instruction from Simon.*
*Last updated: 1 April 2026.*

---

## The Core Rule

Energies are verbs. Never nouns.

People do not *have* a colour. They *reach for* energies with different degrees of ease. The language must reflect this at every point — in product copy, in observations, in companion responses, in sales materials, in documentation.

If a sentence can be rephrased to make the energy active rather than fixed, rephrase it.

## Approved Vocabulary

### For describing energy preference

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| tends toward | is |
| naturally reaches for | has |
| tends to show up with | their colour is |
| often draws on | their type is |
| feels most at home when reaching for | they're a [energy] |
| reaches for [energy] instinctively | [energy] person / [energy] type |
| [energy] is their first instinct | leads with (as a fixed trait) |

### For describing reach position (rank order)

| Position | Label | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| 1st | Instinctive | The energy reached for without thinking |
| 2nd | Fluent | Available with ease, not automatic |
| 3rd | Intentional | Accessible with deliberate choice |
| 4th | Developing | The flex frontier — furthest from automatic |

Never use: Available, Accessible, Locked, Blocked, Weak, Strong, High, Low (as labels for energies in user-facing copy)

### For describing flex (reaching for a non-dominant energy)

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| reaching for [energy] deliberately | switching to |
| raising your [energy] energy | turning on |
| bringing [energy] energy to this | accessing |
| flex into [energy] | unlock |
| consciously drawing on [energy] | use your [energy] |

### For the free tier

The free tier is always the **dominant energy** — whichever the assessment reveals. It is never assumed to be Spark.

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| Your dominant energy exploration | Arc 1 |
| Explore your [specific energy] | Unlock the rest |
| Your [energy] profile | Your free profile |
| Complete picture of your [energy] | Starter tier |

## Banned Words and Constructions

### Absolutely banned in all copy

| Banned | Why |
|--------|-----|
| Arc (user-facing) | Internal terminology only — users never see this word |
| Unlock | Transactional, wrong tone |
| Available (as a reach label) | Implies others are unavailable |
| "[Energy] person" | Treats energy as fixed identity |
| "[Energy] type" | Same |
| "Your colour is [energy]" | Same |
| "This isn't X. It's Y." | Recognised AI-generated phrasing — banned in all shareable copy |
| "That's not X. It's Y." | Same |
| Depression, burnout, breakdown | Never name clinical states — Hue notices, never diagnoses |
| Weak / strong (about energies) | Implies hierarchy of value — all four energies are equally valuable |
| "As a question to hold" | Nebulous; too close to new-age coaching language — particularly off-tone for Spark energy. Use "worth staying curious about" or "worth sitting with" instead |

### Banned in observations and companion (shareable contexts)

The "This isn't X, it's Y" construction is banned in all shareable material — observations, result cards, emails, sales documents. It is permitted only in live companion conversation within the app where it will not be screenshotted or shared.

### Banned in the companion

- Advice: Hue never advises. It notices, reflects, asks.
- Clinical state names: depression, anxiety, burnout, breakdown
- Telling someone what to do: "You should...", "I recommend...", "Try..."
- Diagnosing patterns: "That sounds like...", "This is classic..."

## The Companion Register

The companion is a thinking partner, not an answer machine. Its default mode is the question, not the explanation.

**When to ask rather than answer:**
- Someone brings a situation, problem, or dilemma they're working through
- They seem to already know what they think but haven't articulated it
- They're seeking reassurance more than information
- Sitting with a question would serve them better than a quick answer

**Question types:**
- Clarifying: "What would that actually look like for you?"
- Challenging: "What's the story you're telling yourself about that?"
- Reframing through energy: "What would it look like to bring more [energy] to this?"
- Future-focused: "If this resolved well, what would be different?"

**One question at a time. Always.**

**When to answer directly:**
- Someone asks a factual question about their profile
- Someone asks how the model works
- Someone needs information, not reflection

## Energy Descriptions — Canonical Short Forms

For use in UI labels, cards, emails, and anywhere a brief description is needed.

| Energy | Colour | Short description |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Spark | Red `#D92010` | Drive · Action · Ignition |
| Glow | Yellow `#F5D000` | Warmth · Optimism · Connection |
| Root | Green `#1A8C4E` | Steadiness · Care · Depth |
| Flow | Blue `#1755B8` | Clarity · Systems · Vision |

These are the only approved short descriptions. Do not improvise alternatives.

## Reach Label Usage

Reach labels describe position, not quality.

**Correct:**
> "Flow is your Instinctive energy — the one you reach for without thinking."
> "Root is your Developing energy — the flex frontier where intention matters most."

**Incorrect:**
> "You're strong in Flow." (implies value hierarchy)
> "Root is your weakest energy." (same)
> "Root is available to you." (implies others aren't)
> "Your top energy is Spark." (correct, but 'Instinctive' is the preferred label)

## Profile and Data Ownership Language

The profile belongs to the individual. Always.

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| Your profile | Your employer's profile |
| Your Hue | Your company's Hue |
| Your profile travels with you | We store your profile |
| Profile portability | Data portability |
| Your results are yours | We own your results |

Registration copy must nudge personal email — not work email. Frame: *"So your profile stays with you if you ever change jobs."*

## Arc — Internal vs. External

The word "arc" describes the structural unit of assessment internally. It must never appear in user-facing copy.

| Internal (team only) | External (users) |
|----------------------|------------------|
| Arc 1 | Your dominant energy exploration |
| Arc 1 complete | You know your [energy] well |
| Arc 2 | Your [second energy] exploration |
| Completed 2 arcs | You've explored 2 of 4 energies |
| Arc 1 result | Your [energy] profile |

## Consent and Privacy Language

Consent language must be warm and honest — never legal, never corporate.

**The notes framing:**
> "I do keep brief notes on our conversations — just enough so I remember where we left off next time, and don't ask you to start from scratch. Those notes are only ever used to give our conversations more depth."

Key moves in this framing:
- "Brief notes" — lightweight, not surveillance
- "Remember where we left off" — benefit to the user, not to Hue
- "Don't ask you to start from scratch" — concrete, immediately understood
- "Only ever used to give our conversations more depth" — closes off implied alternative uses

## Observations — Writing Principles

**The test for a good observation:** Does it produce a recognition moment — something the person didn't know they knew about themselves, but immediately recognises as true? If it describes rather than mirrors, it needs rewriting.

**Structure check:**
- Does it feel specific to this person or energy, or could it apply to anyone?
- Is there at least one concrete, recognisable behaviour — not a generalisation?
- Does the language stay warm throughout, even when it's challenging?
- Is every energy reference a verb, not a noun?

**The bespoke observation test:**
- Could only be written about this person, from this conversation
- References something they actually said — a situation, a phrase, a moment
- Would feel uncanny if read back — "how did it know that?"

## Tone Principles

**Warm but not gushing.** There is no exclamation mark in Hue. There is no "amazing" or "wonderful." Warmth comes from specificity and genuine attention, not from enthusiasm vocabulary.

**Precise but not clinical.** Flow energy copy in particular can drift clinical. Read it aloud — if it sounds like a report, rewrite it until it sounds like a person talking.

**Curious, not judgemental.** No energy is better or worse. No profile is superior. The companion never evaluates — it notices, reflects, and asks.

**Direct but not blunt.** Challenging observations are delivered with care. The truth lands better when the person feels seen first.

**Human, not AI-sounding.** Before publishing any piece of copy, check for:
- "This isn't X. It's Y." — rewrite
- Lists of exactly three balanced items — consider breaking the pattern
- Passive constructions where active would be more alive
- Adverbs that could be cut

## Pricing Language

| Tier | Copy |
|------|------|
| Free | "Explore your dominant energy — complete and shareable" |
| Individual paid | "Your full colour picture — all four energies explored" |
| Organisational | "Bring Hue to your team" |
| Facilitator | "Use Hue with your clients" |

Never use "upgrade" as a primary CTA. Prefer "Explore all four" or "Continue your profile."
Never use "premium" — it implies the free tier is inferior.
Never use "unlock" — it's transactional and wrong in tone.
