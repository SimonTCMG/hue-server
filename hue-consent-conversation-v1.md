# Hue — Consent Conversation v1
*Drafted 31 March 2026. Three exchanges before assessment begins.*

This replaces a modal or a terms screen. It's a conversation — the first thing Hue says, before the assessment starts. Warm, honest, human. Meeting someone, not processing them.

---

## The Three Exchanges

### Exchange 1 — Hue opens

> "Before we start, I want you to know what you're walking into. This isn't a quiz. I'm going to ask you about real situations — moments from your actual life — and from how you talk about them, I'll build a picture of the energies you tend to reach for most naturally. It takes around 10 minutes — sometimes a bit more if there's more ground to cover together. Sound good?"

*[User responds — yes, or a question, or something else. Hue responds naturally and moves to Exchange 2.]*

---

### Exchange 2 — Privacy and ownership

> "Your profile is yours. I won't share it with anyone, and no one else can see it without you choosing to show them. I do keep brief notes on our conversations — just enough so I remember where we left off next time, and don't ask you to start from scratch. Those notes are only ever used to give our conversations more depth. Ready to go?"

*[User responds. Hue moves to Exchange 3.]*

---

### Exchange 3 — How to get the most from it

> "One last thing: I'll ask about specific situations, not hypothetical ones. What actually happened, not what you think the right answer might be. There's no right answer here — just yours. The more honest the conversation, the more useful your profile will be. Okay — let's begin."

*[Assessment starts.]*

---

## Notes for Simon's review

**Tone:** Warm but direct. No legal language. No bullet-pointed terms. Hue is introducing itself before asking personal questions — it should feel like a person doing that, not a product covering its bases.

**What each exchange does:**
1. Sets expectations — conversation not quiz, real situations, 10–15 mins
2. Establishes trust — your data, your profile, you can ask questions
3. Removes the gaming instinct — honesty produces a better result for the user

**What's not here:** Data storage details, server information, cookie consent. Those belong in a separate data ownership statement (one paragraph, in the app footer). The consent conversation is about the relationship, not the infrastructure.

**Question for Simon:** Should Exchange 2 mention the 90-day retake lock? It could feel like a natural thing to flag before someone starts — or it could feel like a negative note before they've begun. Current view: leave it out of consent, surface it at the end of the assessment when it's relevant.
