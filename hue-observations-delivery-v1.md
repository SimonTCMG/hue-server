# Hue — Observations Library: Usage Instructions for Code
*To be read alongside `hue-observations-v1.md`. Governs how and when observations are served in the app.*

---

## Two Delivery Contexts

Observations are delivered in one of two ways: **automatically in the initial profile**, or **dynamically in the Hue companion conversation**. Never all at once. Never as a reference document for the user to browse.

---

## Context 1 — Initial Profile (Post-Assessment)

Served automatically when the results screen is generated. Selection is determined entirely by the user's scores. Three types are triggered:

### 1. Reach-position observations (observations 1–16)
One per energy, based on that energy's ranked position in the user's profile.
- 1st-ranked energy → Instinctive observation for that energy
- 2nd-ranked energy → Fluent observation for that energy
- 3rd-ranked energy → Intentional observation for that energy
- 4th-ranked energy → Developing observation for that energy

All four are served in the initial profile, ordered by rank.

### 2. Profile shape observations (observations 68–73)
Triggered conditionally based on score data. Check in this order:

| Observation | Trigger condition |
|---|---|
| 68. All four energies low | All four scores below [threshold — to be defined] |
| 69. All four energies high | All four scores above [threshold — to be defined] |
| 70. Wide gap highest to lowest | Gap between 1st and 4th score exceeds [threshold — to be defined] |
| 71. One energy near-zero | Any single score below [threshold — to be defined] |
| 72. The power of lower energies | Always serve — applies to every profile |
| 73. All four near-equal AND high | All four scores within close range AND all above [threshold — to be defined] |

Observation 72 (the power of lower energies) is the only one served universally — it applies to every profile and should always appear.

### 3. Most relevant misread observation (observations 29–36)
Serve one misread observation based on the user's 1st-ranked energy:
- Spark instinctive → serve either 29 (aggression) or 30 (impatience) — select based on conversation content
- Glow instinctive → serve either 31 (naivety) or 32 (pushover)
- Root instinctive → serve either 33 (disengagement) or 34 (slowness)
- Flow instinctive → serve either 35 (coldness) or 36 (indecision)

If conversation content doesn't indicate a preference, default to the first option for each energy.

### 4. Near-equal combinations (observations 61–67)
Triggered conditionally — only if two or more scores are within close range of each other. Serve the relevant pairing observation. If multiple pairings qualify, serve the one involving the 1st and 2nd ranked energies.

Do not serve observation 67 (all four within close range) from this group — that is covered by the profile shape logic above.

---

## Context 2 — Hue Companion Conversation

These observations are **not served upfront**. They are held in reserve and surfaced by the companion when the user's message makes one directly relevant. The companion does not quote the observation verbatim — it uses the observation as the basis for its response, expressed in natural conversational language.

### Observations held for companion use:

| Group | Observations | When to draw on them |
|---|---|---|
| Two-energy pairings | 17–28 | When the user describes a dynamic that involves their top two energies interacting — decisions, relationships, team situations |
| Under pressure | 37–40 | When the user describes a high-pressure situation, conflict, or moment where they didn't show up as they'd have liked |
| In leadership | 41–44 | When the user asks about leading, managing, or influencing others |
| In conflict | 45–48 | When the user describes a disagreement, difficult conversation, or relationship tension |
| In relationships | 49–52 | When the user describes a personal or close working relationship |
| Flex crossing moments | 53–60 | When the user is actively trying to reach for a non-dominant energy, or asks how to show up differently |

The companion should surface at most one observation per response. It should feel discovered, not delivered.

---

## What Is Never Surfaced Automatically

- The full library is never presented as a browsable list
- Two-energy pairings are never shown in the initial profile (scores alone are not sufficient accuracy; these become reliable only once both energies have been explored in depth across multiple arcs)
- Under pressure, in leadership, in conflict, in relationships observations are never in the initial profile — they require conversational context to land correctly

---

## Bespoke Observation (Second API Call)

In addition to the selections above, the initial profile includes one observation generated by a second API call immediately after the assessment. This observation is drawn from the specific conversation — a moment, phrase, or pattern unique to this user. It cannot be pre-written and is not part of the observations library.

Prompt for this call:
*"Based on this assessment conversation and these scores, write one specific observation about this person that could only be written about them — drawn from a specific moment, situation, or phrase they used. 2–3 sentences. Warm, precise, second person. Don't name the energy directly. Don't use the 'This isn't X, it's Y' construction."*

This observation should appear prominently in the results screen — it is the highest-value moment in the initial profile.

---

## Accuracy Note — Arc Completion

The reach-position observations (1–16) are generated from Arc 1 scores. They are accurate enough to be remarkable for a first profile. As the user completes further arcs, scores may shift — observations should be refreshed to reflect updated rankings. The companion should be honest with users that a single-arc profile is a strong first picture, not a final one.
