Visual system · v1

We are born with our anian.

The four energies orbit a still centre. The centre is you. This is what that looks like — across the mark, the profile, the state, and the team.

One — the mark

The Y stays still. The colours move around it.

The Y is drawn — two arms reaching up and outward from a grounded stem, meeting at a soft junction below the centre. Not a typeset letter. A gesture. The four energies orbit around it at the cardinal positions: top, right, bottom, left.

No energy is at the top of a hierarchy. No energy is at the centre. The centre is the person. The energies move around them — present, accessible, in relation. This is Kawai's hollow centre rendered in motion.

Two — the wordmark

One name. One mark. Always together.

Pronounced AN-yan, from the Welsh word anian — nature, temperament, and power, held in one concept. On dark and coloured surfaces, the Y renders in cream so the centre stays luminous against the orbiting colours. Each colour dot carries a thin outline — ink on every surface except dark ones, where the outline turns cream — so each dot stays cleanly defined wherever it lands.

Three — the four energy icons

A small visual language for each energy.

The four icons live alongside the energy names wherever the framework needs to be felt rather than read. Lightning for Spark. Radiating circles for Glow. A leaf for Tend. A double wave for Flow. Each rendered in its own colour, sitting in a soft pill of its energy's light tint.

Four — the profile

Not a chart. A territory you've come to know.

Every other personality tool gives you four bars stacked against each other — a hierarchy, with one colour at the top. The Anyan profile shows your cynefin: the territory you have come to know. Soft edges. No walls between energies. The whole hillside is yours; this is just the part you tread most often.

The small dot in the territory marks the centre of home — where you spend the most time, on average, when nothing is asking you to flex.
Five — the state layer

The same person. A different day.

The dashed shape is your cynefin — the territory you've come to know. The solid shape is how you're showing up today. Under pressure or in a different context, the territory shifts. This is the Ayurvedic distinction: prakriti beneath, vikriti on top. Neither replaces the other.

Six — the team

The awen of a team is more than the sum of its profiles.

Each translucent shape is one person's cynefin. Together, they make a collective texture — where the team concentrates, where the ground is thin, what flows between them. This is what a team buyer sees. Never individual data. Never raw percentages. Just the shape of what the team is.

Six people. Four energies. A shape that belongs to no one of them, and to all of them.

Each dot is one person's centre of home. Each soft territory is the ground they tread most often. Where the territories overlap, the team has natural reach. Where they thin out, the team has territory to walk into deliberately.

One person here lives at the far end of Spark — their cynefin reaches up where no-one else's does. Without them, the team's collective shape would barely touch that part of the hillside. This is what diversity looks like in the awen: not balance, but reach.

No deficit. No gap radar. Just the team's cynefin, made visible — and the conversation that flows from seeing it.
