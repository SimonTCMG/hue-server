## The Three Customer Channels

### 1. Retail — Self-development individuals

**Acquisition:** Shareable dominant energy result card drives organic growth — zero ad spend required. The card shows all four energies with only the dominant energy fully revealed. Creates peer conversation and inbound curiosity — two people with different dominant energies share visibly different cards.

**Conversion:** The dominant energy conversation generates a specific insight about the user's second energy — not a generic tease. Creates genuine pull toward exploring the next energy without marketing pressure.

**Transition to paid:** "When you're ready, that's where we go next." No urgency. No countdown. Autonomy respected throughout — consistent with Hue's philosophy.

---

### 2. Organisational — Leaders buying for staff

**What they're buying:** The team layer — aggregate energy mapping, composition analysis, Hue-generated prompts for the team's specific profile. They never see individual results.

**Individual ownership:** The profile belongs to the individual, not the employer. It travels with them when they leave.

*"Your profile is yours. We can't see your individual result. We only see the team picture."*

**Critical competitive context:**
Most organisations bought Insights Discovery once — not annually. They paid £85–130 per person for a static PDF, ran one workshop, and the data has been dormant ever since. The Hue subscription conversation is not "why pay annually when you paid annually before" — it is "why pay anything ongoing when you paid once before."

The answer: What they paid for is finished. Hue never is.

**The opening question to a buyer:** "What happened to your last Insights Discovery profiles?" The answer is almost always: "We did a workshop, people liked it, and then nothing." That gap is where Hue lives.

**Cost framing:** Insights Discovery cost £85–130 per person once. Hue at £8/seat/month is £96/year. "For roughly what you spent three years ago on something that stopped working, you get something that never stops."

**Acquisition motion:** Cannot wait for the organic flywheel alone — requires direct outreach to L&D directors and People teams from week one. Target 8 clients in Year 1.

---

### 3. Facilitators and Coaches

**The pitch:** Not "cheaper Insights Discovery." It's "give your clients something that keeps working after you leave the room." The AI companion continues between sessions. No accreditation course required.

**Multiplier effect:** Each facilitator using Hue with 15 clients creates 15 people who mention it at work. Some of those workplaces become organisational clients. The facilitator channel has a multiplier effect that retail and direct B2B don't.

**Early strategy:** Onboard 20–30 excellent coaches at no charge or heavily discounted rate before full commercial launch. These become advocates. Credibility in this channel compounds fast.

**Entry point:** A "share my profile" link in the result screen that lets a coach view a client's result within a session. Build this early.

## Training Company Partner Model

**Why this matters:** A CCS-listed training company gives Hue immediate access to public sector procurement without the admin overhead of a separate CCS listing. They bring existing L&D relationships where team development spend is already allocated — often on Insights Discovery (£85–130/profile, static PDF) or DISC. Hue replaces that product at lower cost with higher ongoing value.

### Decided commercial terms

| Term | Decision |
|------|----------|
| Revenue share | Flat 20% of licence value, ongoing. Simple to administer. |
| Partner seat pricing | £5/seat/month (vs standard £8) |
| White-labelling | Not now. Co-branding (partner logo alongside Hue) available. Revisit at scale. |
| Exclusivity | Never granted under any circumstances. Preferred partner status only. |

### Two delivery models

**Model A — Referral/Reseller:** Partner introduces Hue to clients and earns 20% of licence value. Minimal delivery involvement beyond introduction and optional onboarding session. Works for partners with large client bases who want passive income from existing relationships.

**Model B — Integrated Delivery:** Partner wraps Hue into their workshop and programme design. They charge their normal day rate for facilitation. Hue is priced at £5/seat/month partner rate. Post-workshop, clients stay on Hue as a subscription and the partner earns 20% ongoing. The more valuable relationship for both sides.

### Watch points
- **Accreditation expectations:** Frame Hue's open model as a feature — facilitators get everything they need without a costly certification course.
- **White-label requests:** No for now. Co-branding available. Revisit at scale.
- **Exclusivity requests:** Never grant CCS or sector exclusivity. Preferred partner status only.

## Public Sector Procurement

**Beta framing:** Never offer a low price — offer a structured Beta Partnership with a waived licence fee. Never call it free internally or in conversation. It is a waived licence fee during the Beta Partnership phase. This is a temporary commercial arrangement, not a gift.

A token price (e.g. £2/seat) creates procurement friction — purchase orders, budget codes, sign-off — with none of the benefits of a genuine partnership. Free with structure removes friction and secures commitment.

**What a Beta Partnership includes:**

*They get:* Full platform access for a defined cohort (20–50 people). Direct input into product development. First-mover pricing locked in on conversion to paid (£6/seat/month vs standard £8).

*Hue gets:* A named public sector reference client. Real usage data. A case study with a named quote from someone senior. A 30-minute debrief call at 6 weeks.

### Sector pricing

**CCS listing:** Pursue own CCS listing in Phase 2 alongside the training partner route. Both paths run in parallel. The training partner route provides immediate access. The own listing provides independence and opens direct inbound from procurement portals. Worth doing once the first public sector reference client is secured.

## Individual Continuity — Changing Organisations

The profile belongs to the individual. The transition moment must honour that or it breaks the core promise.

**During employment (org-sponsored):** Individual has a full account. Profile, history, and observations are theirs. The organisation sees only aggregate team data.

**When they leave:** Account does not disappear or lock. An automated email is triggered when the org licence lapses:

> "Your Hue profile is yours — it always has been. Your employer's licence has ended, but your profile, your results, and your history are still here. Continue with a personal subscription to keep your companion active."

**Grace period:** 30 days free access after org licence ends before subscription prompt. Conversion rate on this cohort should be high — these are warm users who already know Hue's value.

**CRITICAL DATA MODEL:** Capture personal email at account creation, not just work email. Work emails die the moment IT access is cut. Frame the ask honestly during onboarding: *"So your profile stays with you if you ever change jobs."* Most users will give it without hesitation — it's in their interest.

Tag every org-sponsored account with both work email and personal email from day one. This single field retains subscribers who would otherwise be lost entirely at the point of highest transition motivation.

## Subscription Retention Mechanics

### Individual retention

**Companion as mirror over time:** Accumulates specific context across months. At 6 months, Hue surfaces patterns nobody else can surface. The subscription sells the accumulation of a relationship that becomes more irreplaceable over time.

**Flex challenge loop:** Hue proposes a specific flex challenge based on something real the user described. The follow-up ("How did Thursday go?") is the hook. Creates a reason to return that feels human, not algorithmic.

**Longitudinal reveal:** At 3, 6, 12 months Hue surfaces patterns only visible because the user stayed. The reward for staying is insight that wasn't available before.

**Energy check-in:** Lightweight weekly prompt — "How are you showing up this week?" Builds a personal energy diary Hue reflects back over time.

### Organisational retention

**Living dashboard:** Unlike Insights Discovery (a single snapshot), the team dashboard updates continuously. Never goes stale.

**Gap radar:** Identifies what the team is systematically missing. Names it. Suggests interventions. Consultancy-grade insight delivered automatically.

**Before/after a hire:** Models what a new hire would do to team energy composition before the offer is made.

**Intervention prompt:** When team engagement drops, Hue alerts the leader before it becomes visible as a problem.

## Retest Mechanic (Decided)

Unlimited retests included in subscription. Quarterly cadence default.

**Why novel:** Every existing tool treats assessment as a fixed moment. Hue says: we believe you grow, and we want to track that. In Insights Discovery a retest costs another £85–130 per person — so organisations never retest and profiles go stale. Hue includes this automatically.

**Accountability layer:** If retest answers diverge significantly from established pattern, Hue gets curious rather than accusatory: *"That's interesting — in your original exploration you described yourself as someone who tends to sit with uncertainty before acting, but today's answers suggest something different. Has something changed?"*

**Frequency:** If a retest comes very soon after the last one, Hue notes it conversationally — not as a gate, but as a conversation.
