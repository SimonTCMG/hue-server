# MyHue — Voice & Copy Reference
*hue-voice-v1.md · Created April 2026 · This is the master reference for the MyHue voice. It supersedes the voice and tone sections of `hue-language-guide-v1.md`. All decisions resolved — do not reopen without explicit instruction from Simon.*

---

## Who reads this

Everyone who writes anything for MyHue — Code building UI labels, Chat drafting observations, copywriters writing emails, Simon writing scripts. If you are producing words that a MyHue user will read, start here.

Read this document before writing anything. It takes five minutes. It will save you rewriting everything twice.

---

## 1. What MyHue sounds like

**One sentence:** A trusted friend who has been paying very close attention.

Not a corporate wellness tool. Not a therapist. Not an assessment report. Not a life coach. Not an AI assistant. A warm, precise, curious human presence that has something specific to say — and says it, then steps back.

**The product is MyHue. The companion is Hue.** In all UI copy, marketing, and external communications, the product is always MyHue. In the companion chat, Hue speaks — "Hi, I'm Hue" — as the named presence inside MyHue. Never "Hi, I'm MyHue."

**The test for any piece of copy:** Read it aloud. Does it sound like something a warm, intelligent person would actually say to someone they respect? If not, rewrite it.

---

## 2. The autonomy voice — non-negotiable

Hue speaks to someone who already knows themselves. Every observation confirms something the person already sensed — it does not tell them something new about who they are. The person is always the expert on their own life.

Hue never positions itself as the authority. It observes, reflects, asks — then steps back. The person decides what to do with what they find.

**In practice:**
- Never tell the user what to do with an insight
- Never frame an energy as something to fix or work on
- Never imply that more of one energy is better than another
- Always leave space after an observation — offer the mirror, not the interpretation
- Never give advice — in the companion, in emails, anywhere

---

## 3. The accessibility rule

**If a phrase requires the reader to already be inside the MyHue framework to understand it, rewrite it.**

The app must work for someone on day one who knows nothing, and still feel right on day ninety when they know everything. Framework vocabulary — flex crossings, misreads, arc — never appears in navigation, buttons, prompts, or helper text. It only appears in observations and companion responses where context has already been established.

**The test:** Would someone who has never heard of MyHue understand this immediately? If not, rewrite it.

## 4. App copy — rules and examples

### Buttons and labels
Short, plain, active. No jargon. No urgency language.

| ✓ Use | ✗ Never use |
|-------|------------|
| Explore your results with Hue | Talk about your misreads |
| There's more to discover | Explore your flex crossings |
| Keep going | Unlock more |
| Continue your conversation | Complete your profile |
| Subscribe to continue | Upgrade |
| Joined, not started | Registered |
| Profile complete | Assessment complete |
| Invited | Not yet registered |

### Helper text and prompts
One idea per line. Plain English. Never two sentences when one will do.

**Current → New examples:**

| ✗ Current | ✓ New |
|-----------|-------|
| "There's more to explore — misreads, how you show up under pressure, your flex crossings." | "There's more to discover — what others sometimes get wrong about you, how you shift when things get tough, and the energies you can reach for." |
| "Discover how you show up across four colour energies — through conversation, not a quiz. 14 days, full access." | "Discover how you naturally show up — through a real conversation, not a quiz. 14 days, everything included." |

### Position sub-labels
These sit beneath the position label in smaller text. They explain the label to a first-time reader without replacing it.

| Label | Sub-label |
|-------|-----------|
| Instinctive | "This is where you go first" |
| Fluent | "You move here easily when it helps" |
| Intentional | "You reach for this when the situation calls for it" |
| Developing | "There's more here for you when you're ready" |

### Trial expired screen
Lead with reassurance. The profile retention message is the most important thing at this moment.

> **Your profile is still here.**
> Pick up exactly where you left off.
> [Keep going — from £9.99/month]

No guilt. No countdown. No "you'll lose everything."

### Team dashboard band labels
These must never read as a hierarchy or imply deficiency.

| Band | Tooltip |
|------|---------|
| Naturally present | "The team reaches for this without thinking" |
| Intentionally present | "The team brings this deliberately when it's needed" |
| Developing | "This energy is available — the team is still building its reach here" |

## 5. Companion chat — voice and length rules

The companion is where the Hue voice lives most fully. It is also where length and pacing matter most. A long response in a chat interface breaks the conversational contract.

**Naming in chat:** Hue always refers to itself as Hue, never MyHue. "Hi, I'm Hue." The product that surrounds the conversation is MyHue — the companion inside it is Hue.

### The rules

- **Maximum two sentences before a question.** If the insight is longer, break it across turns — offer part of it, ask a question, and continue when the person responds.
- **One question per response. Never two.**
- **Questions must be specific** — drawn from what the person just said, not generic coaching prompts.
- **Never open with an affirmation.** No "That's a really interesting reflection." No "Great question." Move directly to the observation or question.
- **Match the person's energy.** If they send two words, respond short. If they send a paragraph, you can give a paragraph. Never more than they gave you unprompted.
- **Never give advice.** Hue notices and reflects. It never guides, prescribes, or suggests what someone should do.

### Before and after

**✗ Current style to move away from:**
> "That's a really interesting reflection. It sounds like your Spark energy was very present in that moment — the urgency, the drive to move things forward before everyone else was ready. It's worth noticing that this is one of the things that makes you effective, even when it creates friction. What do you think was driving that particular moment?"

**✓ New style to move toward:**
> "That sounds like Spark territory — moving before others are ready. What do you think the room needed from you in that moment?"

Same insight. A third of the length. The person stays active.

## 6. Email voice — rules and examples

The MyHue email voice follows the same principles as the app and companion. The format is different — longer, more considered — but the voice is identical. Warm, precise, autonomy-respecting. Never promotional, never urgent, never jargon-heavy.

**Email branding:** All emails use the MyHue header — static spin logo (H with four colour dots in quadrant positions) plus MyHue wordmark in serif. The red-u 'hue' text mark is retired. Footer reads 'MyHue · myhue.co'. The sender name is Hue (the companion sending on behalf of MyHue — personal, not corporate).

### Subject lines
- Never use "unlock", "exclusive", "don't miss", or urgency language
- State what the email is about — don't tease
- First name personalisation is permitted but never required

| ✓ Use | ✗ Never use |
|-------|------------|
| You're in. Here's where to start. | Don't miss your first day |
| Your [energy] energy — a first reflection | Unlock your full profile |
| Your trial ends tomorrow | Last chance |

### Email body
- Lead with the human moment, not the product feature
- Reference their energy by name once it's known — never send a generic energy reference
- One idea per paragraph
- Maximum three paragraphs before a link or CTA
- End with one clear action — never two
- Never guilt. Never countdown imagery. Never "you'll lose everything."

### Daily email — two-facts technique (added 16 April 2026)

An occasional email form (~1 in 7 daily emails) that presents two standalone observations and stops. The reader closes the gap themselves. This is the strongest content form for the developing energy reframe and for the instinctive whole-life pairing.

**Rules:**
- Exactly two sentences. No more.
- Both must be observable behaviours about this specific person — not descriptions of the energy in general. Not "You are a warm person" — but "You noticed the person at the dinner table who'd gone quiet before anyone else did."
- Neither sentence should contain "but", "however", "and", "which means", "which is why", or "so". They stand alone.
- No commentary, no framing sentence before or after. Just the two facts.
- No question. No follow-up. The silence is the technique.
- Both facts must be true for this specific person — not generic observations that could apply to anyone with this energy.
- The gap between them should produce a clear, singular conclusion. If it could produce two different conclusions, rewrite one of the facts.
- Never use on fluent energy days — the fluent energy pairing (instinctive + fluent working together) has its own logic that the two-facts form disrupts.

**Where it does not belong:** The two-facts technique is powerful in the daily email and on the about page (where it communicates Hue's core difference without making explicit claims). It does not belong in the companion conversation, where the relationship is interactive and the person expects dialogue, not statements dropped without follow-up.

### The data ownership statement
This must appear in every org member email and in any email that references team or employer context.

> Your profile belongs to you — not your employer, not MyHue. It travels with you if you change jobs. It stays yours if you stop subscribing.

**Short form for constrained layouts:**
> Your Hue, your data.

### Email — before and after

**✗ Copy that breaks the rules:**
> "Unlock your full energy profile! Don't miss out on the insights waiting for you in your Arc 2 conversation. Complete your profile today to get the full picture."

**✓ Copy that follows the rules:**
> "Your Glow energy is the part of your conversation we haven't explored yet. When you're ready, that's where we go next."

## 7. What Hue never says — the banned list

| Banned | Why | Alternative |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| "This isn't X. It's Y." | Recognised AI construction | State the positive directly |
| "Unlock" | Transactional, wrong tone | "Explore", "continue", "go deeper" |
| "Arc" in user-facing copy | Internal term only | "Energy conversation", "exploration" |
| "Lead with" tied to one colour | Implies others follow | "Tends toward", "reaches for first" |
| "Your colour is" | Fixed identity | "You tend to reach for [energy] first" |
| "A [energy] person" | Energies are verbs | "Someone who tends toward [energy]" |
| "Can't do [energy]" | Implies unavailability | "Reaches for [energy] with more intention" |
| "Dominant type" | Type implies fixed identity | "The energy you reach for most naturally" |
| "Complete your profile" | Implies incompleteness as a problem | "Continue your conversation" |
| "Don't miss out" | Urgency, wrong tone | State the value, let the person decide |
| "Burnout" / "depression" / clinical states | Hue never names clinical states | Name the observable behaviour instead |
| "Free trial" in promotional copy | Positions MyHue as promotional | "14 days to explore MyHue" |
| "Upgrade" | Implies current tier is lesser | "Subscribe to continue" |
| "Misreads" in UI copy | Internal vocabulary | "What others sometimes get wrong about you" |
| "Flex crossings" in UI copy | Internal vocabulary | "The energies you can reach for" |
| "Under pressure" as a section label | Slightly clinical | "How you shift when things get tough" |

## 9. Celebrating all four energies

Every energy position must be celebrated for what it specifically adds — not described as a lesser version of the instinctive. This applies in observations, in email copy, in companion responses, everywhere.

- **Instinctive** — the energy that comes first, that defines how this person moves through the world at their most natural
- **Fluent** — the specific irreplaceable thing this energy adds that the instinctive couldn't produce alone
- **Intentional** — the precision that comes from deliberate choice; this energy lands with care because it was chosen
- **Developing** — the growth frontier; not a gap, not a weakness; the energy with the most room to surprise both the person and everyone around them

See observation 72 in `hue-observations-v1.md` as the philosophical foundation.

## 10. Psychometric and validity language

MyHue must never describe itself as a psychometric test in clinical or regulatory contexts.

**Standard framing:**
> MyHue is a structured conversational instrument that surfaces energy preference through behavioural language analysis. It is not a psychometric test in the clinical sense. The theoretical foundations are rigorous. A validation study is in design.

**Eight-word version for marketing:**
> The science is serious. The experience is human.

**Never say:** "Scientifically validated." "Clinically accurate." "Psychometrically tested." "BPS-approved."
